Triads

A chord is made up of three or more notes played together, all built from a single starting note called the root. In this lesson, we focus on triads, which consist of three notes:

  • Root
  • Third
  • Fifth
A triad stacks the root, third, and fifth.
The same chord can also be shown on a piano keyboard.

Major triads

A major triad is formed by adding a major third and a perfect fifth above the root. To build a C major triad, start on C, then count 4 half steps up to E and 7 half steps up to G. The triad is C–E–G.

  • Start on C
  • +4 half steps → E
  • +7 half steps → G

For an E♭ major triad, begin with E♭ as the root. Count up 4 half steps to get G, and 7 half steps to get B♭. The triad is E♭–G–B♭.

  • Start on E♭
  • +4 half steps → G
  • +7 half steps → B♭

Minor triads

Minor triads are built differently: they use a minor third and a perfect fifth. To build a C minor triad, start on C, count 3 half steps up to E♭, and 7 half steps up to G. The triad is C–E♭–G.

  • Start on C
  • +3 half steps → E♭
  • +7 half steps → G

For a B minor triad, start on B. Count 3 half steps up to D and 7 half steps up to F♯. The triad is B–D–F♯.

  • Start on B
  • +3 half steps → D
  • +7 half steps → F♯

General rule

  1. Start on the root
  2. Choose major (4) or minor (3) for the third
  3. Add the fifth (7)
